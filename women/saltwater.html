Seasonal Cute

Saltwater

Saltwater is elemental beauty at its most unapologetic. She did not arrive at this beach — she belongs to it. The references are real and specific: the raw confidence of a Sports Illustrated cover shot on an empty stretch of coast, a Baywatch one-piece worn like armor, a string bikini on a woman who could swim a mile in open water without thinking about it. Everything about her is sun-earned and salt-real — the tan, the hair, the effortless way she inhabits whatever she is wearing. The palette is warm and coastal: deep turquoise, coral, sun-bleached white, wet sand, ocean blue, burnt gold. Fabrics are minimal and functional — they get wet, they dry in the sun, they move with the body rather than against it. Cover-ups are worn over swimwear or alone with equal confidence. She is not posing for anyone. She just happens to look like that.
