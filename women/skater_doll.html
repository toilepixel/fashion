Skater Doll

Skater Doll takes everything the skate park gave us and runs it through a hyper-feminine filter until it comes out pink and breathless and somehow still dangerous. The cargo pockets are still there, but now they're on a micro pleated skirt in baby pink with a little bow at the waistband. The graphic tee still has attitude, but it's cropped to ribbons and probably mentions Hello Kitty or a band she loves ironically. The knee-high socks have lace at the cuff. The platform Mary Janes replaced the Vans, though she'd still know how to ride in them. The palette is aggressively pastel — cotton candy pink, lavender, baby blue, mint, and soft white — shot through with pops of cherry red and the occasional black detail that winks at the dark origins. Bows appear on everything: the belt, the hair, the sneakers. It is absolutely, deliberately a lot, and she owns every inch of it with the smug ease of a girl who learned long ago that being adorable and being formidable are not mutually exclusive.
