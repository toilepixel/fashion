Electric Floor

Electric Floor is what happens when disco sheds its nostalgia and becomes something alive and entirely now. The 70s are not the reference — movement is. Every piece is chosen for how it behaves when the body moves: liquid fabrics that ripple under club lights, stretch sequin that catches every photon, fluid silhouettes that flare and flow with the dance rather than against it. The palette is nocturnal and electric — deep midnight, hot gold, liquid silver, champagne, rich jewel tones that pulse under colored light. This is not costume disco and not retro pastiche — it is the pure spirit of a woman who dresses for the dance floor and owns every inch of it. The night has a full arc here: the pre-party shimmer of getting ready, the dance floor at full intensity, and the specific magic of 2am on a city street still glowing, jacket thrown over a slip dress, absolutely alive. She is not performing joy. She is made of it.
