Athletic Seasonal

Shore Thing

Shore Thing lives in the water, on the water, or five minutes from it — and every inch of her shows it. This is surf culture filtered through genuine athleticism and a total indifference to looking like she's trying: board shorts worn low over a bikini top, a rash guard pushed up at the sleeves, hair salt-stiffened and sun-bleached, everything slightly faded from a hundred washes in hard water. The palette is the ocean itself — deep turquoise, coral, sandy beige, warm white, sun-faded indigo — with the occasional flash of neon wax or retro surf print. Fit is relaxed but never sloppy; there's a lean, physical ease to everything she wears. Off the board, the look shifts slightly but doesn't transform: a cut-off denim skirt thrown over a bikini, a vintage surf tee knotted at the waist, bare feet or worn slide sandals. Sun-warmed, salt-kissed, and entirely in her element.
