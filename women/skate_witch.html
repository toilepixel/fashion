Skate Witch

Skate Witch is what happens when the skate park meets the occult and neither one blinks. The palette is almost entirely black — matte, glossy, shredded, and draped — broken only by deep crimson, electric purple, and the cold gleam of silver hardware. Fishnet underlayers peek through ripped tights and torn fabric. Chains hang from belt loops. Boots are heavy, laced to the knee, and non-negotiable. The silhouette is a confrontational mix of volumes: a skin-tight top under a shredded oversized tee, a micro skirt over ripped tights, a massive jacket with nothing underneath worth mentioning. Graphic tees bear dark imagery — skulls, pentagrams, band logos that mean something. The mood is abandoned warehouse at midnight, neon sign flickering, absolutely no regrets. She doesn't perform danger — she simply is it, in the quiet, certain way of someone who has never needed anyone's approval and stopped pretending otherwise a long time ago.
