Skater Girl

Skater Girl is the real thing — not the costume version, but the girl who actually grew up at the skate park and developed her style there without thinking about it too hard. Baggy cargos sit low on the hips over a pair of Vans or battered Nikes. The crop top is either a faded graphic tee knotted at the front or a tight ribbed tank that just happened to end up there. The overshirt is always open, always oversized, always slightly stolen from someone. The jeans, when she wears them, are wide-leg or straight-leg and sat at the hip, not the waist. There is nothing precious about any of it — the clothes take a beating and look better for it. The palette runs washed-out and sun-faded: bleached denim, dirty white, faded black, olive, orange, and the occasional loud graphic that pops against all that low-key base. She is effortlessly cool in the way that only comes from genuinely not caring, and the whole look is charged with a loose, physical confidence that no amount of deliberate styling can fake.
