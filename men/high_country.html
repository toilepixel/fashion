High Country

High Country is what a man wears when the terrain is the point. This is not the polished outdoorsman of a catalogue shoot — it's the real thing: technical layering systems that actually work, worn-in hiking boots that have seen serious mileage, base layers that wick and mid-layers that insulate and shells that keep the wind out. The aesthetic is built on function but arrives somewhere genuinely handsome through the quality of the materials and the earned patina of real use — a fleece with a fraying cuff, a pack with a replaced buckle, a flannel soft from a hundred washes. The palette is the mountain environment itself: stone grey, pine green, burnt amber, slate blue, warm brown, and the occasional flash of safety orange on a jacket or pack strap. Off the trail the look doesn't transform — a Merino base layer and worn chinos, a fleece left open, boots that could take you back up the mountain tomorrow if you asked them to.
