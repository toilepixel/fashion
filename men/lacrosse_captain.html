Preppy Athletic Character

Lacrosse Captain

Lacrosse Captain is the guy who has been dressing this way since high school and genuinely doesn't think about it. His wardrobe is a uniform — not in the military sense but in the tribal one: the clothes of a particular kind of American man who went to a particular kind of school and has never really left. OCBD shirts in white and pale blue, chinos in khaki and navy, a Barbour jacket that belonged to his father, loafers worn without socks from April through October. The fit is always correct without ever being fashion-forward — nothing too slim, nothing too baggy, everything where it's supposed to be. Layering is instinctive: a crewneck sweater over a collared shirt, a blazer thrown on for dinner. The palette is classic and slightly faded — navy, hunter green, burgundy, tan, white, heather grey — colors that have been worn by men like him for generations and will be worn for generations more. He is handsome in an unthreatening, wholesome way. He holds doors. He knows which fork to use. He has never once thought about his aesthetic because he has never had to.
