Weekend Dad

Weekend Dad is the quietly aspirational everyday man — not a fashion person, but somehow always put together. He's wearing well-fitted chinos or dark jeans, a good flannel or a faded Oxford shirt with the sleeves rolled up, broken-in leather boots or clean white sneakers, and a jacket that's seen enough use to look earned rather than purchased. The aesthetic draws from classic American weekend dressing — workwear, prep, and outdoors — worn with just enough ease to feel genuinely lived-in. The palette is warm and grounded: navy, olive, burgundy, camel, washed denim, heathered gray. Nothing is ironed to perfection; nothing is wrinkled either. The mood is a Saturday morning with coffee and nowhere to be for an hour: a farmers market, a backyard, a hardware store run that turns into a long conversation. He's not trying to look good. He just does.
