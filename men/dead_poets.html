Dead Poets

Dead Poets is the man still searching — not yet arrived, not yet settled, and the better for it. His wardrobe is collegiate and slightly undone: worn tweed jackets with patches at the elbow, wool turtlenecks in oxblood and forest green, corduroy trousers that have sat through too many late seminars, Oxford shoes that have walked autumn quads in the dark. Everything fits as though it was bought slightly too large and has been lived in since. The layering is instinctive rather than considered — a scarf thrown on, a blazer over a sweater over a collared shirt, an overcoat grabbed at the door. The palette runs dark and warm and literary: oxblood, aged brown, forest green, dark navy, camel, ink black, dusty gold — the colors of old books and older buildings. He is emotionally intense and intellectually restless, the kind of man who annotates his novels and argues about things that matter at hours when he should be sleeping. Close kin to the Weathered Gentleman but younger and less resolved — where that man has made his peace, this one is still in the thick of becoming. The mood is dark wood library, rain on stone, a desk lamp at 2am, autumn light through tall windows.
