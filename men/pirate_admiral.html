Pirate Admiral

Pirate Admiral is the golden age of piracy at its most theatrically magnificent — not the grubby deckhand, but the captain who has accumulated enough plunder to dress like the navy officer he robbed. The look is a chaotic but magnificent layering of stolen finery and hard use: a heavy wool naval coat with gold epaulettes (acquired, not earned), a linen shirt open to mid-chest, knee breeches tucked into tall bucket boots worn soft. The palette is the Caribbean and the horizon — midnight navy, storm grey, black, deep crimson, tarnished gold, ivory, and the occasional flash of emerald green. Rings on every finger, a pistol in the sash, a cutlass at the hip, and a hat with just enough plume to make a point. The look communicates that this man has no law but his own, and the freedom suits him.
