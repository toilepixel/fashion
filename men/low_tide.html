Low Tide

Low Tide is the guy who actually surfs — not the one posing beachside with a board he rented once. Everything he wears has been in the water, dried in the sun, and worn again without a second thought. Board shorts sit low on the hips, rash guards get pushed to the elbows, and the single concession to style is a vintage surf tee that's been washed so many times the print is barely there. His hair does what salt water tells it to. The palette is the beach at its most honest — sun-faded navy, warm sand, bleached white, coral, washed-out olive — nothing bright that didn't start out brighter. Off the water the look barely changes: maybe a linen shirt thrown open, worn slides replacing bare feet. He's lean, unhurried, and completely indifferent to being watched. The whole aesthetic reads like a long summer that never quite ended.
