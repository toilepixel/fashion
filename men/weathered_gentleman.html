Weathered Gentleman

Weathered Gentleman is the man who has been somewhere interesting and never quite left. His clothes carry the quiet authority of lived experience — heritage tweeds that have softened with age, wool overcoats with the weight of good winters in them, leather boots that have earned their creases. Nothing is new. Nothing is trying. Everything fits with the easy confidence of a wardrobe built slowly over years rather than assembled in an afternoon. The silhouette is generous without being shapeless: slightly roomy in the shoulder, straight through the leg, layered with intention. A scarf worn properly. A watch that belonged to someone else first. The palette is drawn from the natural world at its most subdued — slate, aged leather, bark brown, faded forest green, oatmeal, ash, deep burgundy — colors that look as though they've weathered real seasons.
