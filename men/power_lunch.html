Corporate Luxury

Power Lunch

Power Lunch is the man who bills by the hour and dresses like it. His wardrobe is a precision instrument — every piece chosen, nothing accidental, nothing soft. Fitted suits in charcoal, navy, and midnight cut close through the shoulder and taper cleanly through the leg. Shirts are always crisp, always white or pale blue, always French-cuffed when the occasion calls for it. Ties are silk, knots are tight, and pocket squares are folded — not puffed. The shoes are always the most expensive thing in the room. This is not inherited wealth dressed down for comfort; this is earned ambition dressed up for consequence. The confidence is practiced and total. He does not check the time — the watch does that. He does not check the bill — someone else does that. The palette is deliberate and unflinching: charcoal, navy, black, crisp white, silver, deep burgundy, subtle chalk stripe. Every color earns its place. The mood is a corner table at a restaurant where the menus have no prices, a conference room on the forty-second floor, a handshake that closes something.
