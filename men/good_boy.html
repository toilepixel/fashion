Good Boy

Good Boy is not dressed so much as he is outfitted — by someone else, for someone else's purposes. The aesthetic is minimal by design: what little he wears exists to expose rather than cover, to signal availability rather than agency. Tight PVC shorts, leather harnesses worn across bare skin, mesh tops that conceal nothing, collars that say everything. The fit is always deliberate and always revealing — waistbands low, necklines open, straps placed where they draw the eye rather than support the body. He is not the one who chose this. The palette runs dark and severe: black, patent black, deep red, gunmetal, chrome — colors of restraint and control. Hardware is everywhere: buckles, rings, clasps, snaps. The mood is dim, private, and entirely out of his hands. His personality descriptors do not include confidence or command — he is eager, compliant, and visibly dependent, his entire bearing organized around the approval of someone who is not him. He is the other half of a dynamic, not a look unto himself.
