Regency Rake

Regency Rake is the dissolute gentleman of early 19th century England — a man of means who dresses with precise, effortful elegance and then smudges it slightly, deliberately. The silhouette is everything: a close-fitted coat with high collar points, a cravat tied in one of forty known ways (his is intentionally imperfect), knee breeches or tight pantaloons that leave nothing to the imagination, and tall polished Hessian boots. The palette is restrained in cut but rich in cloth: midnight blue, deep claret, bottle green, ivory, champagne, black, and the occasional dangerous dash of canary yellow. The tailoring is Savile Row before Savile Row had a name. He is not a dandy — the dandy is ostentatious; the rake is seductive. The difference is that the dandy wants to be looked at; the rake wants to be wanted.
