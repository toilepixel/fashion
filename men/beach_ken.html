Everyday Seasonal Character

Beach Ken

Beach Ken is performing preppy — knowingly, joyfully, and with maximum effect. Where Lacrosse Captain never thinks about his clothes, Beach Ken thinks about nothing else. The palette is the first clue: pink trousers, lime green blazers, coral shorts, turquoise polos, madras in four colors at once. Everything coordinates because he made it coordinate. Everything is slightly too much because slightly too much is exactly the point. The silhouette is relaxed and sun-kissed: linen blazers worn over bare chests, chinos rolled to show bare ankles above loafers, polos left untucked on the boat. He is Palm Beach more than New England, California more than Connecticut — the preppy tradition filtered through sunshine and self-awareness and turned up to a cheerful eleven. There is a wink in everything he wears. He owns pink trousers unironically and would be genuinely puzzled by the idea that they need defending. The mood is a country club pool at noon, a yacht with too many people on it, a rooftop in July. He is attractive in a golden, easy, slightly ridiculous way, and he knows it, and that is most of the charm.
